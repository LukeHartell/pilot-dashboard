About Pilot Dashboard

Last updated: ...

Pilot Dashboard is a free, open-source web app designed to help pilots plan, log, and analyze their flights. It is built with a focus on simplicity, usability, and data privacy.

Vision

A world where every pilot - glider, powered, or ultralight - can confidently plan, fly, and log their journeys using one userfriendly platform that works with them, not against them.

Mission

To empower pilots with a seamless, modern platform that simplifies every step of flying - before, during, and after - through trusted tools, intelligent insights, and total data ownership.

Data & Privacy

Pilot Dashboard respects your privacy and security above all else.
All personal data is stored exclusively on secure, in-house Danish servers. No personal or flight information is ever shared with third parties, unless you explicitly choose to do so (for example, exporting your logbook or backup to Google Drive).
Your data remains your data: always exportable, always under your control, never held hostage or sold.
For full details, see our Privacy Notice and Terms of Service.
You can request a copy of all your data here at any time.

Core Values

Value What It Means for the Platform
Pilot First Every feature is useful in the cockpit, on the field, or at home.
Simplicity by Design Clean UI, no ads, no unnecessary clicks. You get what you need.
Transparency & Trust Your data is yours. Always exportable, with possibillity for local backup. So if Pilot Dashboard ever disappears, at least your logbook didn't go down with it.
AI as a Co-Pilot Smart insights enhance your judgment. You stay in control.
Inclusive Designed for SPLs, PPLs, UL pilots, and students alike.
Sustainable Freemium Pilot Dashboard will forever remain completely usable completely for free with many preflight features not even requireting a login. Pilot Dashboard is a passion project, not a business.

Roadmap

Pilot Dashboard is a work in progress and is currently in early stage of development. Some features are available in beta. Feel free to test, create an account, and explore. However, many functions are missing or not implemented yet, and you might run into bugs.
